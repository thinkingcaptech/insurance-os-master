# Technology Stack & Tools (2025 Guide)

## Executive Summary
- 2025 mandate: aggressive, AI-driven efficiency plus strict compliance/auditability (TCPA one-to-one consent; CMS/Best Interest).
- Core choice: all-in-one vs best-in-breed. Recommended hybrid: back-office AMS hub (policy/commissions/compliance) + front-office best-in-breed (dialer, quoting, scheduling, marketing).
- Consent/compliance reshape value of dialers/CRMs; auditable one-to-one consent and call recording become non-negotiable.
- Convergence: lead management, marketing automation, and dialers absorbed into CRM/AMS; pick ecosystem, not silo tools.

## Key Platform Takeaways
### CRM/AMS
- **General CRMs**: Salesforce (max customization, long/expensive implementations, rich ecosystem); HubSpot (marketing-first, strong free tier, big jump at Pro).
- **Specialist AMS (Back-Office)**: AgencyBloc (best for policy/commission admin; limited integrations; simple mobile), NextAgency (+NextCommission, Zapier, transparent pricing), AgencySmart (all-in-one).
- **Front-Office Suites**: Better Agency (all-in-one, sales automations, mobile weak), iLife (sales OS, universal e-app/quoting, walled garden), Shape (built-in phone/email/text).

### Dialers
- Choose **power dialer** (quality + compliance) over predictive. Required features: DNC scrubbing, consent logging, call recording, time-zone filters, local presence.
- Mojo (power, single/triple line, ~$149/user with add-ons), PhoneBurner (power, unlimited, $140–$183/user with CRM/automation/AI notes), MightyCall (preview/progressive/predictive, budget $30/user on Power).

### Marketing Automation (if not bundled)
- HubSpot Marketing Hub (best-in-class all-in-one).
- ActiveCampaign (powerful visual automation, built-in CRM/lead scoring, budget-friendly).
- Mailchimp (easy, weaker automation; good for simple newsletters).

### Quoting / Illustration / e-App
- iPipeline (LifePipe + iGO): industry standard for Term/GUL; wide carrier network; native e-app.
- Ensight: compliance-first, multi-product “single-entry” illustrations (Term, GUL, WL, IUL, VUL, Hybrid/Trad LTC) with auditable Best Interest process.
- Trend: universal e-Apps (iLife; custom Salesforce portals).

### Communication & Scheduling
- Video: Zoom (best client-facing), Teams/Meet if already on 365/Workspace.
- Scheduling: Calendly (great free for solo), Acuity (flat-fee better for teams).
- Compliant texting: Beetexting, ClientCircle (HIPAA/TCPA, A2P 10DLC).
- Client portals: Moxo for secure doc/task flows.
- Internal comms: Teams (Microsoft stack) or Slack (best integrations/search for non-Microsoft).

### LMS / Training
- Skillcast (compliance content), SmarterU (license/cert tracking), ProProfs (free for very small teams).

### Analytics & Commissions
- Sales KPIs from CRM/dialer; network rollups via AgencyKPI (for FMOs).
- Commissions: Use AMS with native tracking (AgencyBloc, NextAgency) or standalone (GreenWave, APL) for complex hierarchies.

### E-Signature
- DocuSign (enterprise, HIPAA/BAA, ESIGN/UETA).
- SignNow (cost-effective, HIPAA/BAA, ESIGN/UETA) for starter/growing teams.

## Risks & Mitigations
- **TCPA one-to-one consent**: Capture/store agency-specific consent; DNC scrubbing; time-zone filters; compliant SMS with STOP; avoid shared leads without proof.
- **Implementation failure**: Phase rollout (hub first), pilot with small team, train before scale; avoid over-customizing early.
- **Data silos**: Pick ecosystem; use Zapier/direct integrations sparingly; centralize source of truth in hub CRM/AMS.
- **Mobile gaps**: Verify mobile needs (Better Agency mobile weak; AgencyBloc simple); plan field workflows accordingly.
- **Compliance (HIPAA/Medicare/Best Interest)**: 10-year call recording/storage; HIPAA/BAA for e-sign/text; auditable illustration (Ensight) for complex cases.

## Action Plan
- **Short-term (0–90 days)**: Choose hub (AMS vs general CRM); enforce consent logging/call recording; pick power dialer with DNC/timezone filters; adopt scheduling (Calendly/Acuity); select e-sign (SignNow/DocuSign).
- **Medium-term (6–12 months)**: Integrate quoting/e-app (iPipeline or Ensight); deploy marketing automation (HubSpot/ActiveCampaign); stand up LMS with compliance content and certification tracking; implement commission tracking (native or add-on).
- **Long-term (1–2 years)**: Evolve to hybrid stack; add AI (call notes, scoring, routing); optimize dashboards for sales + persistency + commissions; periodic compliance audits of consent/recordings/BAAs.

Dialer Systems: Amplifying Outbound ProductivityFor any agency conducting outbound prospecting, a dialer is a mandatory efficiency tool. The strategic choice is not just which dialer, but what type of dialer.Strategic Selection: Power vs. Predictive DialersThe decision between a power and predictive dialer has significant implications for both productivity and compliance.44Power Dialer: Dials one number at a time, sequentially, for a single agent.46 The agent is on the line and hears the "hello" when the prospect answers.Pros: This allows for more control, personalization, and time to review contact information before the call connects.45 It poses a lower compliance risk as an agent is always present, preventing abandoned calls. This method is explicitly identified as ideal for industries like Insurance and Banking where interaction quality is high.46Cons: Lower overall call volume compared to a predictive dialer.45Predictive Dialer: Uses an algorithm to dial multiple numbers per agent simultaneously.44 When a live person answers, the dialer instantly connects them to the first available agent.Pros: Maximizes agent "talk time" and is optimized for high-volume, low-conversion call centers (e.g., telemarketing).44Cons: High risk of "abandoned calls" (when a person answers but no agent is free). This creates a significant TCPA compliance risk.45 It can also create a poor customer experience, described as for when you "don't need to provide a great experience".46For a life insurance agency, where trust and a high-quality, personalized interaction are paramount, a Power Dialer is the clear strategic choice. The marginal efficiency gain of a predictive dialer is not worth the compliance risk or the negative first impression.45The Compliance Mandate: 2025 TCPA "One-to-One Consent"This is the most critical development for any agency that buys leads.
